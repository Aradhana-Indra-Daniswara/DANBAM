Chef Eun Hoon Yoshiro

A 38 years-old chef that started it all. He opened DanBam in Indonesia on the 17th of August 2004. He only started with 5 of his friends to cook alongside him during the 2 years DanBam opened.

A Failure ★☆☆☆☆

When the restaurant officially opened, the food was sub-par. everything was inconsistent, sluggish, and not cooked properly. For 6 months, Yoshiro has sleepless nights, regreting the decision of opening DanBam.

A Man Who Lost Everything

It's like putting a salt on a wound. While he was stressed out with DanBam, his father died at the age of 75. He was desperate, lost, nowhere to talk to. He started to borrow some money to his friends, but none are willing to help.

Risen From The Ashes

From the inheritance of his father, He started creating his own recipes, his own twist on korean cuisine. Out of 120 dishes, he only picked 40 to be put on the menu. And what he achieved, is astonishing. The dishes are so delicious that the restaurant is fully booked the night he finished his recipes.

Attention to Details

He stayed disciplined, focused. He's willing to give it all for the restaurant. If you taste his food, you will see nothing is out of place, everything has a purpose on the plate, and most importantly, it tells a story.

Talented Chefs

After a couple of months, He picked up the very best and determined chef. They want the chefs to tell their own story with the food they create, giving a wonderful harmony in the restaurant. He wants the customer to taste the different culture, minds, and creativity between the chefs

100% Fresh Ingredient

He chose to use all fresh ingredient for Danbam, "I want the mistakes to only either be the tools, or the chefs", said Yoshiro. The chefs agreed and they enjoy buying groceries every morning.

Great Food

After all that hard work and money that he put out, it paid off. DanBam earned it's first michelin stars in april 1st, 2019. And his passion for cooking has been re-lit.
